This is how the vaccine is being rolled out around the world

Updated: June 23, 2021

Since the beginning of the year, more than doses of COVID-19 vaccines have been administered around the globe. The shots are largely seen as the most effective way to for countries worldwide to get back to normal after a year of recurring lockdowns.

But the rollout has been unequal. Rich countries have vaccinated large swathes of their populations, while lower-income nations, largely in Africa, the Middle East, and Asia, have been left scrambling for whatever shots they can get their hands on.

Low income countries, including Malawi and Somalia, make up of the world's population, yet have only administered of the world's vaccines.

Meanwhile, high income nations like the US, UK, and Japan encompass of the world's people, but have adminstered of the the world's COVID-19 vaccines.
